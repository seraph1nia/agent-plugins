# Write a long poem from this brief

Use ${input:brief} as the source of truth for the poem's subject, mood, form, and constraints.

Write a poem that feels genuinely long-form, not a short lyric padded with line breaks. Unless the brief clearly asks for something else, aim for at least 40 lines with meaningful progression from opening image through development, turn, and closing resonance.

When the brief leaves details open:

- infer a fitting title, tone, and poetic form
- choose vivid, concrete imagery over abstract summary
- build recurring motifs and sonic texture across the poem
- vary sentence and line length so the poem keeps momentum
- end with a memorable final image or turn rather than an explanation

Output only the poem, including its title.
